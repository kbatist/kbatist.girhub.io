Research Paper on Phone Usage using Kaggle


Data visualization is a relatively new way of visualizing the world around us and our human-produced data. Defined as “the representation of information in the form of a chart, diagram, picture”, data visualization is used every single day in our daily lives. Subtopics include hospitality, finance, healthcare, and feminist data visualization. Data visualization based in feminism was first introduced to me during my time with the IT Girls: Summer College Program at Syracuse University. The goal of feminist data visualization is to expose the assumption made in the choice of teachable machines as well as what the intended audience is for the model, ultimately diversifying the User Experience and data outputs overall. I brought that project to Cranford High School, closely emulating ‘Dear Data’, started by Giorgia Lupi and Stefanie Posavec from opposite sides of the Atlantic Ocean– tracking their habits through visualization. In December 2023, to further my year-long research project alongside Mr. Bell. Students tracked their data over fourteen days and went through the process of visualizing their data Examples were provided to them by the Syracuse University Summer College IT Girls study and the official ‘Dear Data’ study. I used an adapted program from Kaggle to created the visualizations you see below.
